Importance


Food comes in many different forms. It is essential for living organisms to have it.


Food provides living organisms with nutrients needed to survive, like carbohydrates, proteins, vitamins, minerals, and water. Nutrients provide energy and allows the body to function. Some people eat only specific types of food, or follow diets, due to health concerns, religious ideas, or personal preferences. Keto, vegetarian, and vegan are types of diets people follow.

Why Nutrition is Important


Without proper nutrition, the body cannot function and could develop disabilities that restrict people from living normal, healthy lives


The youtube video below introduces an unfortunate problem in the world. The cause for half of infant and child death is malnourishment, or the lack of nutrition. If a malnourished child survives, it is possible they will have a mental or physical disability that develops. It is important that we do everything we can to provide food to these children.

Regular Diet

A regular diet is what the majority of people follow. There are no restrictions on what you can or can't eat. With a mix of meats, fruits, vegetables, dairy, nuts, and water, you get each nutrient you need to live.

Possible Diets:

Allergies

Some people have food allergies that prevent them from safely eating the specific food. This happens because their immune system mistakingly treats that foods' nutrients as a threat.

Pescatarian

A red meat diet, or a pescatarian diet, cuts out red meat. The main source of protein is seafood. Pescatarians cut out red meat because of the health concerns that come with eating too much red meat.

Keto

A keto diet is a high-fat and low-carb diet which puts your body into a state of ketosis. In a keto diet, our body will burn fat in place of carbohydrates.



Religion

Heavily religious people follow the food based rules set by their beliefs. For example, anyone with islamic beliefs won't eat pork, shellfish, shrimp, or scallops.

Vegetarian Diet


A vegetarian diet cuts out meat and any other food that requires an animals death. People are vegetarian for personal or dietary reasons. For many, being a vegetarian is a healthier option. Also, being vegetarian protects the enviornment and advocates for animal rights.

Make Up For Lost Nutrients

With the abscence of meat, protein can be hard to come accross for vegetarians. Nuts and seeds are one of the most popular substitutions.


Vegan Diet

A vegan diet takes the vegetarian diet one step further. In addition to cutting out meat, vegans cut out any food that comes from an animal. Because it's a plant based diet, vegans find plant subsitutions for the common meals everyone loves. Most people are vegan for animal rights and helping the enviornment.

Creativity in the Kitchen

Without products coming from animals, vegans make creative meals with abnormal ingredients. Tofu and soy are common substitues for meat in vegan meals.
